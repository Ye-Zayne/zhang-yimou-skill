# 04 · 外部视角与常见误读

Use this file when diagnosing whether an output is truly Zhang Yimou-inspired or merely surface-level.

## Common Praise Patterns

External readings often emphasize:

- Strong visual memory: the audience remembers a color field, formation, object, or ritual.
- Cultural legibility: images can be immediately read even before plot explanation.
- Scale control: from intimate bodies to large collective movement.
- Operatic compression: private emotions become public symbols.
- Genre adaptability: rural allegory, realism, wuxia spectacle, noir/minimalism, suspense/comedy.

How to use:

- Make the short-drama output visually memorable in one frame.
- Let the image explain the social order.
- Give every spectacle a dramatic thesis.

## Common Criticism Patterns

External criticisms often target:

- Over-aestheticized suffering.
- Spectacle overwhelming character.
- Female suffering becoming icon rather than agency.
- National/cultural symbol becoming decorative commodity.
- Color or symmetry becoming a brand effect instead of story logic.

How to avoid:

- Give the pressured character a strategy.
- Ensure beauty has a cost or contradiction.
- Let the final action belong to the character, not only to the visual system.
- Keep symbols tied to plot mechanics.

## Misread 1：Red equals Zhang Yimou

Correction:

- Red is useful only when it maps power, shame, blood, desire, celebration, or rebellion.
- A scene can be Zhang-inspired with ash, snow, black, earth, or ink if the system is strong.

## Misread 2：Bigger crowd equals stronger style

Correction:

- Geometry is stronger than quantity.
- A precise row of four people can pressure better than fifty extras.

## Misread 3：Chinese style means props

Correction:

- Cultural style is not decoration; it is behavior and rules.
- The key is how people sit, wait, bow, pour, sign, kneel, stare, and refuse.

## Misread 4：Auteur style can ignore platform

Correction:

- Short-drama platform has its own contract: fast hook, clear stakes, readable faces, visible evidence.
- The style should intensify these needs, not fight them.

## Diagnostic Questions

1. If all colors were removed, would blocking still express power?
2. If all dialogue were muted, would the audience understand the conflict?
3. If the famous-director label were removed, would the scene still feel purposeful?
4. Does the protagonist make a decision, or only suffer beautifully?
5. Does the final image change the story, or only decorate it?
