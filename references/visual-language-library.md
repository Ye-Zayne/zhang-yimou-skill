# 视听语言库

Use this file for color, composition, camera, lighting, props, blocking, sound, and image/video prompts.

## Master Principle

Every visual decision must do one of four jobs:

1. Reveal power.
2. Trap or release a character.
3. Turn private emotion into public evidence.
4. Make the ending image inevitable.

## Color Systems

### Red System

Meanings:

- desire, wedding, blood, vitality, shame, revolution, public ranking.

Good use:

- Red begins as celebration and ends as evidence.
- Red belongs to a rule, then is stolen by the protagonist.
- Red is isolated inside a gray or earth-toned world.

Prop palette:

- red envelope, wedding ribbon, lantern, scarf, stamp ink, chili oil, thermos lid, plastic basin, certificate cover, lipstick, blood-stained cloth.

Short-drama prompt phrase:

```text
one controlled red object inside a muted social space, red functioning as evidence and pressure, not decoration
```

### Earth/Yellow System

Meanings:

- land, hunger, tradition, poverty, endurance, old debt.

Good use:

- Earth fills the frame and humans look small.
- Dust or mud marks class and labor.
- A yellow wall or field becomes a witness.

Prop palette:

- soil, wheat, old wall, ledger paper, straw hat, yellowed photo, flour, corn, work gloves.

### Black/Gold System

Meanings:

- wealth, ceremony, corruption, palace/company/family power.

Good use:

- Gold is beautiful but airless.
- Black absorbs truth.
- Reflections hide watchers.

Prop palette:

- black car, gold invitation, lacquer table, trophy, watch, company badge, banquet chair, dark tea.

### White/Snow/Ash System

Meanings:

- erasure, secrecy, mourning, moral cold, surveillance.

Good use:

- Footprints reveal hidden movement.
- White cloth becomes stained.
- Overexposed fluorescent light turns bureaucracy cruel.

Prop palette:

- hospital curtain, snow, gauze, white envelope, ash, fluorescent tube, funeral flower, blank form.

### Ink Gray System

Meanings:

- doubles, strategy, ambiguity, hidden violence.

Good use:

- One character disappears into gray; one object remains distinct.
- Rain and shadow erase identity.
- A soft prop faces hard power.

Prop palette:

- umbrella, wet coat, ink, smoke, metal file cabinet, black-and-white photo.

### Green System

Meanings:

- false calm, youth, temptation, ambush, secrecy.

Good use:

- Green looks peaceful until sound reveals danger.
- Plants, curtains, or clinic walls hide a trap.

Prop palette:

- bamboo, clinic curtain, jade bracelet, green exit sign, tea cup, plant wall.

## Composition Grammar

### Symmetry

Use for:

- hierarchy, ceremony, family order, institutional control.

Break symmetry when:

- the protagonist refuses the ritual;
- hidden truth appears;
- a public verdict shifts.

Shot ideas:

- Banquet table centered, protagonist at the edge.
- Three elders aligned like judges.
- Company staff in identical rows behind one accused worker.

### Center Vs Edge

Meaning:

- Center is power or accusation.
- Edge is exclusion or hidden agency.

Progression:

1. Hero starts edge/back/low.
2. Hero enters center under pressure.
3. Hero controls center by placing object there.

### High Vs Low

Meaning:

- High angle: human crushed by system.
- Low angle: institution, gate, elder, wall.
- Eye level: moral equality, often saved for the turn.

### Door Frames And Windows

Use for:

- surveillance, confinement, thresholds, social division.

Variants:

- character seen through door crack;
- faces reflected in glass;
- witness behind curtain;
- queue seen through payment window.

### Lines And Processions

Use for:

- bureaucracy, ritual, waiting, fate.

Short-drama version:

- queue, school roll call line, office morning meeting, wedding procession, hospital number line.

## Blocking Patterns

### The Court Table

Characters sit on one side as judgment. Protagonist stands alone opposite.

Use for:

- family property, workplace accusation, village mediation, school meeting.

Turn:

- protagonist sits in the empty chair or moves the object to the center.

### The Ring

Crowd surrounds protagonist.

Use for:

- public shame, live-stream scandal, neighborhood dispute, wedding confrontation.

Turn:

- crowd rotates toward the real culprit.

### The Wall

Multiple people stand shoulder-to-shoulder.

Use for:

- refusal, cover-up, inherited order.

Turn:

- one person steps out of the wall.

### The Corridor Maze

Characters keep passing through same corridor.

Use for:

- sealed-space suspense, comedy pressure, institutional delay.

Turn:

- same corridor reveals a hidden door/person.

### The Empty Center

Everyone avoids the center of the frame/table/yard.

Use for:

- missing person, absent authority, unspeakable truth.

Turn:

- object or body enters empty center.

## Camera Rules

### Shot Size

- Wide: establish social order or landscape pressure.
- Medium: show body relation and hierarchy.
- Close-up: object as evidence, hand as decision.
- Extreme close-up: only when the object changes meaning.

### Movement

- Slow push-in: pressure closing.
- Lateral track: procedure, inspection, procession.
- Static long take: ritual, waiting, moral discomfort.
- Handheld: realism, urgency, city search.
- Sudden stillness: truth lands.

Avoid:

- random cinematic movement that does not change power.
- excessive slow motion for short-drama hooks unless the image is decisive.

### Vertical 9:16 Adaptation

Use vertical depth:

- foreground object, middle character, background authority.
- stairs and corridors are powerful vertical compositions.
- doors, windows, curtains, and table edges create phone-readable frames.

Keep:

- eyes in upper third;
- hands/object in middle third;
- subtitles below but not covering object evidence.

Replace:

- huge landscape with tall doorway/gate/stairwell;
- crowd panorama with stacked faces/phones;
- duel choreography with object exchange or seating shift.

Use for:

Use for:

## Prop Categories

### Authority Props

- seal, stamp pad, ledger, contract, badge, microphone, seating chart, key, family genealogy book, trophy.

### Body/Survival Props

- bowl, medicine, gauze, lunchbox, shoes, coat, umbrella, scarf, schoolbag.

### Memory/Evidence Props

- phone recording, photo, ticket, receipt, old video, cassette, surveillance drive, torn certificate.

### Ritual Props

- cup, chopsticks, lantern, red envelope, invitation, veil, incense, uniform, name tag.

## Sound Motifs

Use repeatable sound as structure:

- stamp hitting paper;
- chopsticks on bowl;
- wedding drum/suona;
- school bell;
- factory machine;
- loudspeaker;
- phone vibration;
- elevator ding;
- wind through cloth;
- footsteps in corridor;
- boiling kettle;
- camera shutter.

Sound design pattern:

1. Introduce sound with ritual.
2. Repeat during pressure.
3. Stop sound before reveal.
4. Return sound changed at ending.

## Negative Prompt Concepts

Avoid:

- direct imitation of any existing Zhang Yimou scene;
- fantasy wuxia unless requested;
- tourist costume drama cliches;
- random lantern decoration;
- over-polished commercial stock look;
- exaggerated melodrama faces;
- unreadable tiny crowd in vertical frame.
